A school trip needs at least one adult to every three children. The coach holds a maximum of 27 passengers. What is the maximum number of children that can go on the trip?
Using a Picture
Here’s a plan of the coach, with the driver’s seat at the front.
To board the coach, you go up the steps at the front and find your seat inside.
We can get rid of the other details and just focus on the passenger seats.
There are 11 double seats, and five seats at the back - 27 seats altogether.
Now fill the coach. Start at the front, with one adult and three children.
Then add another adult, and another three children.
Carry on in the same way, until the coach is nearly full.
There are three seats left. There are already three children for every adult, so we need one more adult.
Then we can put children in the last two seats. That makes seven adults and 20 children.
Now fill the coach. Start by adding one adult and three children.
