Remainders
There are 27 seats on the bus. How many groups of four (one adult and three students) will fit on the bus?
We need to divide 27 by four: 27 ÷ 4 = 6 remainder 3.
Six groups of four leaves three empty seats. We need one more adult, and two students.
We need seven adults altogether, and 20 students can go on the trip.

A rope that is 20 metres long is cut into three equal pieces. How long is each piece?
20 ÷ 3 = 6 remainder 2 (or 6.333... on a calculator.) You cannot buy part of a notebook, so the answer is simply six. Six notebooks cost £18, and you get £2 change.
Try this question. It's the same calculation as last time: 20 ÷ 3. But what will you do with the remainder this time?
A dog walker is allowed to be in charge of a maximum of three dogs. What is the minimum number of dog walkers needed to walk 20 dogs at the same time?
Now the calculator answer 20 ÷ 3 = 6.333... makes sense. You should give the answer to an appropriate level of accuracy, so 6.33 metres would be a sensible answer.
A final example. It's still 20 ÷ 3, but how would you handle the reminder now?
20 ÷ 3 = 6 remainder 2 That meant:6 notebooks6.33 metres7 dog walkers
Six walkers can look after 18 dogs. One more dog walker will be needed to look after the two remaining dogs - so seven dog walkers will be needed altogether.
