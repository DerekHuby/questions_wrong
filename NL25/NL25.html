You can think about filling the bus with groups of four people - with three children and one adult in each group. It's a division problem, because we can start by working out how many groups of four fit in the bus.
Four into 27 goes six times, with a remainder of three. The key to this problem is to work out what to do with the remainder.
We can put six complete groups of four on the bus - that is 18 children and six adults. The remainder of three tells us that there are three seats left. What can we do with those?
There are already three children to every adult on the bus, which is as many as we are allowed. We need to add another adult. There are then just two seats left, and we can put children in those.
So, six groups of four, with three children in each group, makes 18 children.The remaining three seats have one adult and two children, so the total number of children that can go on the trip is 20.
