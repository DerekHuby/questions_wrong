A notebook costs £3. How many notebooks can you buy for £20?
20 ÷ 6 = 6 remainder 2 (or 6.333... on a calculator.) You cannot buy part of a notebook, so the answer is simply six. Six notebooks cost £18, and you get £2 change.

Lots of problems are like this - a calculation gives an 'answer' (like 27 ÷ 4 = 6 remainder 3), but we then need to work out what that answer means. In this example, the remainder means 'add one more adult and two more children'; but in 'More about the Maths' you will see examples where the remainder is handled differently.
When we solve a real-world problem like this, we turn it into a calculation - we go from the 'real world' of buses and seats to the 'maths world' of numbers and operations.
We can do our calculations in 'maths world' – but it's important to go back to the 'real world', and decide what the results actually mean.
